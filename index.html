I am currently delving into the world of research, pursuing a research based master's degree in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Moratuwa in Sri Lanka, with a keen focus on speech processing. My journey began with a bachelor's degree in Electronic and Telecommunication Engineering, also from the University of Moratuwa.

During my undergraduate studies, I gained professional experience through internships at Wave Computing and Paraqum Technologies. Prior to embarking on my master's journey, I honed my skills further at Axiata Digital Labs as a Software Engineer.

Presently, I am immersed in research aimed at creating a unified voice model for speech, speaker, and emotion recognition, leveraging multi-task learning. Past endeavors include developing algorithms for light field video compression using approximate discrete cosine transformation, alongside research into vision based autonomous drones for GPS-denied environments.

My research interests lie in the realms of signal processing, particularly in speech processing, and machine learning, specifically in natural language processing.
